Shade · Must-have

Neso Grande Beach Tent

An absolute game-changer for Hawaii — you will not regret it. Traditional beach umbrellas are a nightmare in Maui's strong afternoon trade winds; they turn into wind-borne spears. The Neso uses the wind to lift itself and anchors down with heavy sandbags, so it handles the breeze beautifully. Plus it packs down tiny into a suitcase — a massive win for travel.

💡 Pro-tip for Maui: Trade winds blow hard on the West Side. Overfill the sandbags until they look like giant heavy basketballs, and pull the ropes as taut as humanly possible before putting the poles in. Also buy a cheap plastic sand scoop (a heavy-duty dog-food scoop or garden trowel) — filling four massive Neso bags by hand is exhausting.

Haul everything

Giant Mesh Beach / Gear Bag

Don't try to haul snorkel gear, wet towels, and kid toys in a standard canvas tote — you'll bring half the beach's sand back into the room. A giant, heavy-duty mesh backpack lets the sand sift out naturally as you walk home, and wet gear can breathe and dry instead of getting moldy.

Required by law

Reef-Safe Sunscreen (check it carefully!)

Hawaii has strict sunscreen laws to protect the coral reefs. Don't buy "reef-friendly" or "mineral-based" blindly — many brands use clever marketing but still contain banned chemicals. Look for Non-Nano Zinc Oxide or Titanium Dioxide as the only active ingredients. Brands like Kokua Sun Care, Raw Elements, or ThinkSport are excellent. Buying a couple of bottles ahead saves a ton vs. ABC Store prices.
