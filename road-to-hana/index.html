Choose your adventure

The Road to Hana is not a normal drive. It is slow, curvy, rainforest-y, waterfall-y, snack-heavy, and very photogenic. Since we are starting from West Maui and our black sand beach reservation is at 3:00 PM, we need to pick one vibe for the day.

Option 1 gives us the best overall Road to Hana experience without pushing everyone too hard. Option 2 is for the family that wants the iconic bamboo forest hike and does not mind a longer, more intense day.

Option 1

Greatest Hits + Short Hikes

Best balance of waterfalls, banana bread, black sand beach, views, and enough walking to feel adventurous.

Option 2

Bamboo Forest Big Hike

The more epic hiking choice. Less stopping, more trail time, later return, bigger bragging rights.

What makes each one special

Option 1's magic

Waterfalls, forests, and snacks

Splash near rainforest waterfalls, walk easy jungle trails, and hit more food stops. Lower effort, more variety, great photos all day.

⭐ Often called Maui's best hike

The bamboo forest tunnel

Pipiwai Trail is widely ranked the best hike on the whole island. You climb through a towering bamboo tunnel, past a giant banyan tree, to Waimoku Falls, a 400-foot waterfall. The most epic, most postable moment of the trip.

So, what should we choose?

Vote based on what sounds more fun, not what sounds more impressive.

Pick Option 1 if
  • You want waterfalls, black sand beach, banana bread, and rainforest without being destroyed.
  • You want more stops and more variety.
  • You want the best food and photo balance.
Pick Option 2 if
  • You really want the bamboo forest hike.
  • You are okay with fewer stops and a later night.
  • You want the most adventurous version of the day.

Reminder: Waiʻānapanapa is the required reservation stop at 3:00 PM. Everything else should work around that.
