THE STORY

She told the truth about her own people — and changed how the West remembers itself.

Born in 1898 in the desert town of Bunkerville, Nevada, Juanita Brooks grew up inside a tight-knit Mormon frontier community, where a woman's road was expected to be settled early. Widowhood, a young son to raise, and a stubborn hunger to know things as they really were set her on a different path.

At mid-life she became one of the American West's most consequential historians. With the 1950 publication of The Mountain Meadows Massacre, she did the unthinkable: she documented a brutal 1857 atrocity carried out by her own community, refusing the comforting story that had stood in its place for nearly a century.

Church leaders pressed her to stop. She kept writing.

A faithful member of her church to the end, Brooks chose history over comfort and integrity over approval. The Ballad of Juanita Brooks is the seven-years-in-the-making account of that life — wife, mother, teacher, and undaunted historian — told as a ballad for the West that made her.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

LINDSAY HANSEN PARK

Lindsay Hansen Park is an American historian, writer, and podcaster whose work makes the complicated history of Mormonism accessible to a wide audience. As executive director of the Sunstone Education Foundation, she has championed open dialogue on faith, identity, and history. Her podcast Year of Polygamy — with more than 16 million downloads — reshaped public understanding of polygamy through rigorous research and powerful storytelling.

She has served as a historical consultant for FX's Under the Banner of Heaven and Netflix's American Primeval, and her writing has appeared in The New York Times, The Washington Post, and the LA Times. The Ballad of Juanita Brooks is the work of seven years.
